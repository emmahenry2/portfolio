About Me

I am recent graduate of the College of William & Mary with a B.A. in History and Environmental Humanities and am currently a graduate student at Northwestern University's Medill School in downtown Chicago where I study Health, Environment and Science reporting.


I am a passionate environmentalist and historian with a long-term goal of pursuing a career in environmental and scientific reporting. I am particularly interested in uplifting stories which emphasize the relationship between Western science and Traditional Ecological Knowledge in indigenous communities across the globe. I believe the best journalists strive for honesty, compassion and meaningful community engagement through written work, multimedia and interpersonal communication.


This portfolio encapsulates my contributions to multiple news outlets and media agencies. Since 2021, I have contributed over 60 articles for various organizations. At W&M, I worked as a Communications Assistant for the Roy R. Charles Center and was the Operations Coordinator for the university student newspaper, The Flat Hat. Outside of W&M, I spent a year as a news intern and general assignment reporter for the Virginia Gazette.


When I’m not reporting, I can be found exploring the outdoors, trying out new coffee shops and cultivating my artistic hobbies!
